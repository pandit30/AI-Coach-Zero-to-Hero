## Question 1 — Type: Concept Check

You have four main image generation tools on the market: DALL-E 3, Midjourney, Stable Diffusion, and Flux. A colleague says "they all do the same thing." Correct them by explaining what each is distinctly best at.

**What to look for:** DALL-E 3 = most "obedient" to the prompt, best at rendering legible text within images, best for straightforward commercial images with text. Midjourney = artistic/cinematic/editorial quality, beautiful but not always literal. Stable Diffusion = open source, customisable, fine-tunable, runs locally, best for enterprise customisation and privacy. Flux = most photorealistic humans, best anatomy (no extra fingers), fastest generation. Students don't need all four perfectly but should get at least three distinct differentiators.

## Question 2 — Type: Application

Your content team wants to create a regional marketing campaign with the same concept executed in 22 different Indian states, each with locally relevant backgrounds. What is the AI-generated image workflow you'd recommend, and what tool would you choose?

**What to look for:** Should describe prompt templating — one base prompt with the variable "background reflecting [state] geography and culture." The session explicitly gives this as a use case: "A bank running a regional campaign needs the same ad in 22 states." Should recommend either DALL-E 3 (for prompt adherence and text-in-image) or Midjourney (for cinematic quality). Should also mention that generating all 22 versions from one template in minutes is the core value proposition vs. the traditional multi-week shoot.

## Question 3 — Type: Scenario

Your CPO asks you to evaluate whether AI image generation can replace the product photography budget for a new D2C clothing brand. What's your honest assessment — what's viable now and what are the limitations to flag?

**What to look for:** Should cite the session's cost comparison: traditional photography ₹50,000/day vs. AI at ₹500 in API costs. Should call out the viable use cases: product backgrounds (inpainting), lifestyle imagery, catalogue variations. Should flag the real limitations: (1) consistent characters across 20 images is hard — every generation varies; (2) hands/fingers are imperfect — check carefully before publishing; (3) text in images is unreliable in most tools except DALL-E 3. Should recommend a hybrid approach: AI for backgrounds and catalogue, humans for hero shots and brand campaigns.

## Question 4 — Type: Concept Check

What is inpainting, and what specific business problem does it solve for food delivery apps like Zomato and Swiggy?

**What to look for:** Inpainting = upload an image, mask a region, describe what should replace it. For Zomato/Swiggy: restaurant-submitted food photos are often low quality with cluttered or ugly backgrounds. Inpainting allows the app to mask the background, type "clean white studio background" or "restaurant table setting," and generate a professional-looking product photo. The session explicitly names Zomato and Swiggy as users of this technique.

## Question 5 — Type: Application

Your legal team asks whether AI-generated images are safe to use in client-facing marketing materials. What is your answer, and which specific tool would you recommend to minimise legal risk?

**What to look for:** Should explain that images generated by models trained on the general web exist in a "grey legal zone" in most countries — copyright law on AI-generated content is still evolving in India and unsettled globally. The safe choice is Adobe Firefly, because it was trained exclusively on licensed stock photos and Adobe Stock, meaning output is copyright-cleared for commercial use. Should also mention that India's copyright law on AI-generated content is still evolving, so caution is warranted beyond just the training-data question.

## Question 6 — Type: Scenario

Your design team is building a tool where ECHO India's clients can generate illustrations for employee training modules. They suggest using Midjourney because "it makes the most beautiful images." What additional consideration should you raise before approving Midjourney for this use case?

**What to look for:** Should identify at least two issues: (1) Midjourney doesn't always follow prompts literally — it interprets creatively — which is a problem when training content needs specific scenarios depicted accurately; (2) character consistency is hard across multiple images for the same course; (3) legal/copyright ambiguity (Midjourney's training data and commercial licensing terms are less clear than Adobe Firefly); (4) for text-heavy training diagrams, DALL-E 3 handles text-in-image better. Strong answers will recommend DALL-E 3 or Stable Diffusion + ControlNet for precision use cases.

---

## Question 7 — Type: Concept Check

The session mentions "ControlNet" as a feature of Stable Diffusion. What is it, and when would a designer choose Stable Diffusion + ControlNet over Midjourney?

**What to look for:** ControlNet is a plugin that adds a second input alongside the text prompt — a structural guide image such as a pose skeleton, edge map, depth map, or rough sketch. It lets the designer control exactly WHERE things appear and in what pose/structure. The key use case: when designers need precise control — specific product shape, specific human pose, specific spatial layout — rather than beautiful randomness. Midjourney is for creative exploration; ControlNet is for precise execution. The session's exact phrasing: "designers use Stable Diffusion + ControlNet rather than just Midjourney — they need precise control, not beautiful randomness."
